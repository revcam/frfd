Accessibility Statement

Last reviewed: May 2026

Filer Rural Fire District is committed to making its website accessible to all members of the public, including people with disabilities. We believe everyone deserves equal access to information about fire safety, emergency services, and community programs.

Our Commitment

We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA — the widely recognized standard for web accessibility. This means we work to ensure our site is:

  • Perceivable — content can be seen, heard, or otherwise sensed by all users
  • Operable — all functions work via keyboard, not just a mouse
  • Understandable — content is clear and behaves predictably
  • Robust — works reliably with assistive technologies such as screen readers

What We've Done

  • Meaningful alternative text on all images
  • Sufficient color contrast between text and backgrounds
  • Keyboard-navigable menus and forms
  • Proper heading structure for screen reader navigation
  • Descriptive link text throughout the site
  • Form fields labeled for assistive technology compatibility
  • Responsive design that works across all screen sizes and devices

Known Limitations

We are a small volunteer fire district with limited resources. While we strive to meet accessibility standards, there may be areas where we fall short. We are committed to addressing any reported issues as quickly as possible. Third-party content such as embedded maps may have accessibility limitations outside of our control.

Compatibility

This website is designed to be compatible with:

  • Modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge
  • Screen readers including NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver
  • Mobile devices and tablets
  • Browser zoom up to 200% without loss of content

Report an Issue

If you encounter an accessibility barrier on this website — or need information provided in an alternative format — please contact us. We take all reports seriously and will work to resolve issues promptly.
